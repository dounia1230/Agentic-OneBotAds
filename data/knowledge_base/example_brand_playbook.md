# Example Brand Playbook

## Purpose

This file gives the assistant reusable positioning, message hierarchy, hooks, CTAs, and communication patterns for Agentic OneBotAds.

This is a starter playbook. Replace any generic assumptions with real positioning once the product site and sales material are finalized.

## Core Positioning

Agentic OneBotAds helps businesses and marketers turn scattered marketing knowledge into a usable execution system.

The value is not "AI for everything."

The value is:
- faster decisions
- reusable knowledge
- more consistent marketing outputs
- less repeated manual work
- safer alignment with brand and platform constraints

## Positioning Summary

- organize knowledge
- improve answer quality
- speed up execution
- reduce repeated manual work
- keep outputs grounded in approved context

## Message Hierarchy

### Primary Message

Marketing work improves when the assistant can use real brand, product, audience, and campaign context instead of generic internet copy.

### Supporting Messages

- a grounded knowledge base improves retrieval quality
- better retrieval improves draft quality
- better draft quality improves execution speed
- reusable context improves consistency across channels

## Hook Examples

- Your ads are not failing because you need more ideas. They are failing because your knowledge is scattered.
- Stop rewriting the same marketing context for every campaign.
- Turn brand docs, product notes, and campaign learnings into answers your team can actually reuse.
- Better prompts are not enough. Better retrieval is the system.
- Give your assistant real context, not generic copy.

## CTA Examples

- See how the knowledge base is structured
- Turn your marketing docs into reusable answers
- Build a grounded RAG layer for your ad workflow
- Centralize brand, product, and campaign context
- Create a more consistent campaign workflow

## Audience Notes

### SME Founders

- care about time, budget, and simplicity
- respond to clarity and visible workflow value

### Agencies

- care about repeatability, reviewability, and speed across accounts
- respond to throughput and consistency language

### Campaign Managers

- care about channel fit, proof, and safe claims
- respond to operational language and clear process

## Tone Rules

- direct
- credible
- efficient
- no inflated claims
- avoid jargon that hides the actual benefit

## Offer Framing Rules

- lead with the operational problem first
- explain how knowledge improves output quality
- do not imply full autonomy where review is still needed
- keep the workflow understandable in one pass

## Inspiration Workflow

Use external ad libraries for inspiration only.

Allowed use:
- message angle research
- CTA pattern research
- creative format research
- offer framing research

Do not:
- copy competitor copy verbatim
- imitate claims without verifying proof
- treat popular ads as automatically policy-safe

## Retrieval Notes

When the user asks for positioning, hooks, or CTA ideas, prefer this file together with brand guidelines and previous ads examples.
