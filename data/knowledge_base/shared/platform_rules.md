# Platform Rules

## Purpose

This file gives broad channel guidance when platform-specific rules are not yet documented for a project.

## LinkedIn

Best for:
- B2B
- founder-led expertise
- service offers
- case studies

Tone:
- useful
- credible
- direct

Avoid:
- clickbait
- exaggerated income claims
- shallow motivational filler

## Instagram

Best for:
- visual products
- lifestyle positioning
- creator and founder storytelling
- short educational sequences

Tone:
- clear
- visual
- warm

Avoid:
- long dense paragraphs
- weak visuals
- captions with no hook

## Facebook

Best for:
- local businesses
- community offers
- mid-intent conversion campaigns
- remarketing

Tone:
- practical
- trust-building
- easy to understand

## TikTok

Best for:
- product demos
- behind-the-scenes content
- founder personality
- quick education

Tone:
- human
- fast
- simple

Avoid:
- ad language that sounds over-produced
- opening with brand history instead of a hook

## Google Search

Best for:
- high-intent offers
- urgent needs
- local services
- direct response

Rules:
- mirror buyer intent
- state the offer fast
- include trust cues close to the CTA

## Cross-Platform Safety Rules

- Avoid guarantees unless legally and factually supportable.
- Avoid unsupported health, income, or personal-attribute claims.
- Avoid fake urgency.
- Avoid copying competitor brand names into headlines or hooks.

## Default Creative Rule

If channel context is weak, prefer clarity, proof, and a low-friction CTA over novelty.
