# Audience Personas

## Purpose

This file defines the audiences most likely to buy Agentic OneBotAds, an AI-assisted ads and marketing operations product.

This is starter content. Validate and refine it with first-party data from:
- GA4 demographic and acquisition reports
- Search Console queries and landing pages
- CRM notes
- sales calls
- onboarding questions
- support tickets
- email objections

## Validation Status

- Status: starter assumptions
- Confidence: medium for broad audience fit
- Confidence: low for exact buying triggers until validated with real customer data
- Review cadence: weekly during active campaign testing

## Persona 1: SME Owner Or Operator

### Profile

- Runs a small or mid-sized business
- Needs more leads or sales without hiring a full internal marketing team
- Prefers simple systems with visible output

### Main Pains

- wasted ad spend
- inconsistent lead quality
- agency fatigue
- lack of time
- unclear reporting
- weak follow-up systems

### Buying Trigger

- "We need better pipeline this quarter."
- "We cannot keep guessing with ad spend."

### Best Messaging Angle

- simplify execution
- reduce manual work
- improve consistency
- keep control visible

### Messaging To Avoid

- enterprise jargon
- hype-heavy AI claims
- vague transformation promises

## Persona 2: Founder-Led Business

### Profile

- Founder still owns growth decisions
- Wants leverage without building a large marketing stack
- Often mixes product, sales, and marketing responsibilities

### Main Pains

- too many disconnected tools
- no repeatable campaign system
- fragmented execution
- weak reporting visibility
- heavy dependence on founder time

### Buying Trigger

- growth is too dependent on the founder doing everything manually
- campaigns stall when the founder is busy

### Best Messaging Angle

- faster campaigns
- clearer process
- reusable assets
- less context switching

### Messaging To Avoid

- overly technical workflow language
- promises that remove the need for review

## Persona 3: Agency Or Freelancer

### Profile

- Delivers marketing services across multiple clients
- Needs speed, repeatability, and clean internal workflows
- Often works with small teams and mixed skill levels

### Main Pains

- content bottlenecks
- campaign setup time
- inconsistency across accounts
- junior team training overhead
- repeated client context gathering

### Buying Trigger

- too many accounts for current team capacity
- delivery speed is becoming a margin problem

### Best Messaging Angle

- systemize delivery
- speed up production
- standardize outputs
- protect quality across accounts

### Messaging To Avoid

- generic "agency growth" claims
- anything that sounds like full automation without human review

## Persona 4: In-House Marketer Or Growth Lead

### Profile

- Owns reporting, experimentation, and channel execution
- Balances leadership requests with campaign operations
- Often needs to justify decisions internally

### Main Pains

- limited time
- pressure for results
- constant request overload
- weak documentation
- repeated explanation work

### Buying Trigger

- leadership wants more output without more headcount
- campaign work is blocked by process overhead

### Best Messaging Angle

- easier to launch
- easier to explain
- easier to reuse
- more grounded internal answers

### Messaging To Avoid

- unrealistic performance promises
- overly casual language for business contexts

## Cross-Persona Patterns

### Repeated Problems

- scattered marketing context
- too much manual rewriting
- weak reuse of past campaign learnings
- unclear safe claims and brand rules
- inconsistency across channels and contributors

### Repeated Desired Outcomes

- faster campaign execution
- more consistent outputs
- better use of internal knowledge
- clearer workflow
- less manual work

## Weekly Validation Inputs

Use these sources to refine persona language and priority:
- GA4 Demographic details and User attributes for aggregated audience patterns
- GA4 User acquisition and Traffic acquisition for source and channel patterns
- Search Console Performance and Insights for query and page language
- Keyword Planner and Google Trends for external demand language and seasonality
- CRM and sales notes for objections and buying triggers

## Retrieval Notes

When the assistant answers persona questions, it should prefer:
- pains before demographics
- buying triggers before vague summaries
- messaging angles before generic audience descriptions

## Web-Scale Audience Signals

With real-time web search capabilities enabled, personas should be dynamically augmented against current search trends:
- If a persona's pain points are evolving (e.g., new ad platform rules causing fatigue), web search will surface these in real-time.
- Combine static persona guidelines with real-time news to ensure maximum relevancy and CTR.
