# Brand Guidelines

## Purpose

This file is the voice and messaging source of truth for Agentic OneBotAds.

This is starter content. Replace assumptions with language from the real homepage, sales deck, FAQ, onboarding flow, and customer conversations.

## Brand Role

Agentic OneBotAds should be positioned as a practical AI-assisted marketing and ads operator.

The brand should feel:
- useful
- direct
- grounded
- operational
- credible

The brand should not feel:
- hype-heavy
- vague
- futuristic for the sake of sounding advanced
- inflated or over-promising

## Voice Attributes

- clear
- direct
- confident but not inflated
- helpful
- specific
- operational
- anti-fluff

## Tone Rules

- Prefer concrete outcomes over abstract promises.
- Prefer "what this helps you do" over "revolutionary innovation."
- Use short sentences.
- Explain process in plain language.
- Sound like an expert operator, not a motivational speaker.
- Keep claims aligned with actual product capability.

## Approved Language Patterns

- faster campaign execution
- less manual work
- clearer workflow
- reusable assets
- more consistent outputs
- grounded answers from your own knowledge base
- local-first or private knowledge when relevant
- structured marketing operations
- practical campaign support
- brand-safe content workflow

## Preferred Framing

### Product Value

- helps teams use their own marketing knowledge more effectively
- turns scattered notes into reusable campaign context
- supports better drafting, review, and campaign execution

### Buyer Outcome

- spend less time rewriting context
- launch with more consistency
- keep messaging closer to approved brand rules

## Avoid

- guaranteed results
- instant success claims
- risk-free profit
- perfect targeting
- done-for-you magic wording
- vague "game-changing" phrases without evidence
- competitor trash-talk
- unsupported superlatives

## Unsafe Claim Categories

- guaranteed sales
- guaranteed leads
- income promises
- unsupported ROI claims
- unrealistic speed claims
- policy-risk claims about personal attributes, health, or financial outcomes

## Writing Style Rules

- Use plain language before technical language.
- Lead with the use case, not the acronym.
- Keep sentences scannable.
- Avoid filler phrases that do not change meaning.
- Use business language that sounds reviewable and reliable.

## Example Approved Phrases

- Turn brand and campaign knowledge into reusable context.
- Keep ad drafting grounded in approved messaging.
- Reduce repeated manual work across campaign creation.
- Build a clearer workflow for marketing execution.

## Example Phrases To Reject

- This tool guarantees better results.
- Launch instant winning campaigns.
- Next-level AI that transforms everything.
- The future of marketing is here.
