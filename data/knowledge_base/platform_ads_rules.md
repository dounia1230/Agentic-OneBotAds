# Platform Ads Rules

## Purpose

This file is the internal summary of policy-safe ad guidance for copy, landing pages, and offer framing.

If there is uncertainty, defer to the official platform policy pages.

## Global Rules

- No false, misleading, or unrealistic claims.
- No guarantees unless they are legally and operationally supported.
- No deceptive landing pages.
- No mismatch between ad promise and landing page.
- No unclear business identity, pricing, or offer terms.
- No exploitative personal-attribute targeting language.
- Keep claims aligned with actual product capabilities.

## Approval Checklist

Before approving ad copy, verify:
- claim safety
- landing page consistency
- pricing clarity
- CTA clarity
- business identity
- platform-specific restrictions

## Google Ads Rules To Remember

- Keep copy concise and relevant.
- Ads, assets, and destinations must be clear, useful, and functional.
- Destination experience matters.
- Misrepresentation and unrealistic claims are policy risks.
- Avoid unsupported superlatives.

## Meta Rules To Remember

- Ads must comply with Meta advertising standards.
- Review copy for prohibited or misleading content before launch.
- Avoid personal attribute assumptions.
- Do not use manipulative urgency unless the offer genuinely supports it.

## LinkedIn Rules To Remember

- Keep copy professional and business-focused.
- Avoid spammy wording, messy capitalization, and low-trust formatting.
- Emphasize business value, workflow value, and decision support.
- Keep hashtags and CTA language relevant and restrained.

## Instagram Rules To Remember

- Use short, visual, easy-to-scan copy.
- Keep the caption clear and readable.
- Avoid cluttered messaging.
- Match visual style with the actual offer.

## Facebook Rules To Remember

- Use friendly and direct messaging.
- Avoid personal attribute assumptions.
- Keep promise and landing page aligned.

## TikTok Rules To Remember

- Check policy constraints carefully for claim-heavy offers.
- Use trend research for inspiration only.
- Do not treat popular creative patterns as automatically policy-safe.

## Safe Framing Patterns

- explain the workflow benefit
- explain the business use case
- use realistic CTA language
- connect the message to a clear next step

## Unsafe Framing Patterns

- guaranteed performance
- instant results
- exaggerated urgency
- perfect optimization claims
- unsupported "best" or "number one" claims

## Retrieval Notes

When the assistant drafts copy, compliance guidance from this file should override aggressive marketing language from other sources.
